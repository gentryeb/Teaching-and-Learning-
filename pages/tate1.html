Tracking Themes in As I Lay Dying

Description:

Students will use the Digital Yoknapatawpha keyword feature to make thematic connections across the text.

Modality:

In-Person

Lesson Plan

Preface

This series of assignments is to be completed once students have grasped the concepts of theme and motif. Throughout these exercises, students will track a theme in As I Lay Dying using quotes that are listed in Digital Yoknapatawpha. They will keep track of these quotes and add quotes of their own that can be added to the database. This will help students who are new to Faulkner grasp a trend in the novel and analyze it through a literary analysis essay which will be composed at the end of the unit. In such a difficult novel, the database, journal, and essay will make Faulkner more accessible to students who are not as well versed in Faulknerian writing.

Activities

Activity #1

Explore
Before beginning As I Lay Dying, practice accessing the appropriate tool we will utilize on Digital Yoknapatawpha (DY) using the following steps:
  1. Go to the Digital Yoknapatawpha Website
  2. Under the search tab, select “Events”
  3. Select As I Lay Dying as the text
  4. Scroll through the “Themes & Motifs” selections. There are many options available. Not all themes and motifs are available for every text one might select.
  5. Select “Absence/Loss.” Page numbers are listed where that particular theme can be found. It is important to note that themes and motifs will possibly be found on pages that are not listed on DY. Therefore, you should be diligent in looking for themes and motifs as you read the novel.
